Social Realism in Philippine Art

Art as a form of resistance and truth-telling

Overview

Social Realism rose to prominence during the Marcos dictatorship, capturing themes of poverty, inequality, corruption, and injustice. Artists like Antipas Delotavo and Pablo Baens Santos painted the truth about life in the margins of society. This movement was both a political act and an artistic movement, aimed at awakening the public and promoting collective resistance.

Key Characteristics

  • Focused on the harsh realities of society, including poverty, labor struggles, and government corruption
  • Used vivid and direct imagery to convey social and political messages
  • Made art a tool for public awareness and activism
  • Resonated strongly with working-class Filipinos, seeking to create empathy and promote change

Notable Artists

Antipas Delotavo

A powerful voice in Social Realism, his works depict the oppression of workers and the underprivileged.

Pablo Baens Santos

His art highlighted the struggles of the Filipino people, often focusing on labor and political themes.
